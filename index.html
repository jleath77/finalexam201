Major League Baseball: The Greatest Sports Organization in the World

Formed in 1903, the MLB is the oldest major professional sports league in the world. Baseball has since grown to many different countries in the world, and a World Baseball Classic is held every four years where these countries get to compete against each other. Many of these players have made their way into the MLB, providing for a diverse and talented group of athletes.

Faces of Major League Baseball today.

Shohei Ohtani

After making a name for himself in Japan, Ohtani made the jump to the MLB in 2018 signing with the Los Angeles Angels. More recently, Ohtani signed a $700 million dollar contract with the Los Angeles Dodgers, making him the highest paid baseball player ever.

Ronald Acuna Jr.

In 2023, Acuna put up one of the most historic seasons ever. He became the first person to ever have a 40 home run, 70 stolen base season, walking his way to a unanimous MVP award waiting for him.

Bryce Harper

As the youngest rookie of the year ever, Harper has had a lot to live up to. With a career of some ups and downs, Harper has found consistent success with the Philadelphia Phillies. He won MVP awards in 2015 and 2021. Needless to say, he is must watch TV.

Marcell Ozuna (From the Braves)

While driving under the influence in 2022, Marcell Ozuna was caught and pulled over. Ozuna tried to get out of any violation by telling the officer that he was "Marcell Ozuna from the Braves". To that, the officer responded and said "So?". However, he leads the MLB in home runs up to this point in the season.
